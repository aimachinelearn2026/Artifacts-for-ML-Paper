accuracy_list = []

for k in range(1, 21):
    knn = KNeighborsClassifier(n_neighbors=k)
    knn.fit(X_train, y_train)
    y_pred = knn.predict(X_test)
    accuracy_list.append(accuracy_score(y_test, y_pred))

plt.plot(range(1, 21), accuracy_list)
plt.xlabel("K Value")
plt.ylabel("Accuracy")
plt.title("K vs Accuracy")
plt.show()

inference: Optimal K-Value: The model performs best with small values of (k). The peak accuracy (approx. 0.94-0.95) occurs when (k) is between 1 and 4. Performance Drop: There is a significant sharp decline in accuracy as (k) increases from 5 to 8, where it drops to roughly 0.825. Stability at Higher K: For (k > 10), the accuracy fluctuates within a lower range of 0.775 to 0.810.

depths = range(1, 11)
train_acc = []
test_acc = []

for d in depths:
    dt = DecisionTreeClassifier(max_depth=d, random_state=42)
    dt.fit(X_train, y_train)

    train_acc.append(dt.score(X_train, y_train))
    test_acc.append(dt.score(X_test, y_test))

plt.plot(depths, train_acc, label='Train Accuracy')
plt.plot(depths, test_acc, label='Test Accuracy')
plt.xlabel("Depth")
plt.ylabel("Accuracy")
plt.legend()
plt.show()

inference: based on the accuracy plot At low depths (below 4), both training and test accuracies are relatively low. The Optimal Depth appears to be around depth 8. This is where the test accuracy reaches its peak (approximately 0.95) and the performance stabilizes. Beyond depth 8, the training accuracy continues to climb toward 1.0, while the test accuracy plateaus. The widening gap suggests the model is starting to memorize the training data rather than generalizing.
